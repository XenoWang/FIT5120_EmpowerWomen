Barriers faced by mothers returning to work
In Australia, the Labour force participation rate for women (62.2%) is significantly lower than for men (71.0%), and women are the primary carer in 52% of households. In addition, more than 41% of women returning to work received a lower rating from their employer and 85% felt they struggled to balance work and life commitments.

"Motherhood Penalty"

The estimate of the "motherhood penalty" implies that having children has a larger impact on women's careers relative to men's.

Flexible Work

Finding positions that offer flexible hours or remote work options can be difficult, especially for those who need to juggle childcare.

Skills Gaps

Rapid changes in technology and industry practices can create gaps in skills or knowledge.

Bias and Stereotypes

Women face bias related to maternity or childcare responsibilities. Stereotypes about mothers in the workplace can affect hiring decisions and promotions.
